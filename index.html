Tilting Game

2021 - 2022

Tilting Game was made as a way for me to make a simple game from my childhood. At the time, I wasn't great at thinking small, so I needed something with an existing foundation. That's when I remembered Labyrinth — a physics-based game built around trial and error, where the goal was simply to reach the end without falling into holes.


Making this game was a good learning experience for me, as it was my first time working in a 3D environment. It introduced me to Blender for asset creation and allowed me to experiment with world-space UI in Unity, a concept I hadn't explored before.


The game itself is solid and, with a somewhat replayable experience designed with speedrunners in mind. It also includes customization which let players change both the ball and the background skybox. While the feature is poorly implemented, figuring out how to add it taught me how to properly implement customization in future projects.

Stepping Stones

2023 - 2024

Stepping Stones was a game I chose to create within unity as a way to pass time while I was undergoing chemotherapy. The develoment was challenging for me, taking roughly 5 to 6 months to complete. It allowed me to learn and understand how to make a game that feels enjoyable and responsive to play.


Though the game's art style is minimalist, I took heavy inspiration from the 2D platformer Downwell, making all the sprites and animations myself while incorporating similar UI/UX designs elements.


The game itself doesn't have much for the player to do other than jumping left to right, but the game is responsive and snappy and has a basic replayability loop. With Players' high scores are saved based on tile height or speed, depending on the selected height. The game also has simple cosmetics that can be unlocked by collecting coins for each jump the player does.

Flicker out

2024 - 2025??

Flicker Out started while I was living in Canada for my gap year before going university. One thing that quickly stood out to me was that Canada had a more prominent smoking culture compared to NZ since tobacco and legalized weed were cheap and easily accessible no matter where you went.


At the time, I had switched from Unity to Godot and was struggling to come up with a solid game idea. Then one day, I accidentally ate a edible cookie, got stoned, and then spent way too long flicking a lighter I had stolen from a friend. At some point, my finger started getting sore, and I began missing flicks. And that's when it hit me - this could be a game.


Because the core gameplay was simple, I chose to test my writing skills to see where it would lead me. Since I couldn't think of a solid concept. I instead wanted to play around the idea of the game having a feel of complexity but once you look into the finer meaning it just shows that it really isn't that important.
